Online: everyone's at war, everything's broken, and the world is *collapsing*.  
Outside: people are walking dogs, chatting over coffee, and *laughing*.

## Entry

A daring life comes head-to-head with being online, with mean world syndrome, with being complacent. How can we get off these damn *phones*? I adore the invention of the word doomscrolling. So obvious in meaning, it condenses down the act of consuming endless negative *unknowns*. 

First coined in 2018 by Ashik Siddique, the term refers to the misery coming from political *activity*. You are what you eat, so we should be mindful about what we consume in digital *captivity*. 

There is something in us that wants to absorb outrage-porn and hatred when we're in that *state*, but to realize this when we're creating suffering for ourselves, is to take back our soul before its too *late*.

"How has the Anthropocene been so far?" I read from John Green's emphatic memoir, while I heard my elderly neighbour shouting fiercely at her dogs next *door*. Humans are imperfect and chaotic, and not everything we do makes sense. It's better to have beautiful foolishness than be cool, if cool means being indifferently tense.

On a few recent flights, I've been surrounded on all sides by people reading *books*. It felt like camaraderie against the doomscroll, albeit 30,000 feet in the air without attention catching *hooks*. 

Instead of spending their time online, going from sweet puppies and kittens, to vitriol and unfathomable *evil*, they were letting their mind wander through pages, working out their brain and boosting cognitive *retrieval*.

> For that time on those journeys, everyone around me was normal, sweet, well-adjusted, and offline. Quite daring, really.

## Exit

I get caught up in self-actualization, but then remember there is a deep therapeutic practice to self non-*improvement*. Sometimes, just sometimes, I can avoid being chronically online, sit with my thoughts, and be aware of their *movement*.

At a certain point, “self-help” becomes self-harm and we become so focused on “optimizing” our lives, that we get filled with *resentment*. Be human. Have unbelievable confidence. Make mistakes. Say the wrong thing. Laugh about it. Sit with *discontentment*.

As Eckhart Tolle says, “Don't seek happiness. If you seek it, you won't find it, because seeking is the antithesis of happiness as a *whole*”. So take life seriously, don't take yourself seriously. The ego should not be in *control*. 

Undershare and do nothing interesting because you're afraid of looking foolish, so the few ideas you do share end up *bland*. Overshare and look stupid, but in the end, no one will care because they're in their own private *dreamland*.

Do difficult things! But remember that creative efforts are killed by hierarchies, metrics, and the fear of *embarrassment*. The more you try to force excellence through pressure and tracking, the more the final outcome loses its *intent*.

You can doom-scroll all you want when you've left your shadow *behind*. Move to a new city, it'll be a humiliation ritual, but one that leads to an *open-mind*. 

For now, maybe, just find a little stoop to prospect and *refuge*, then dream big, then bigger, until the idea is too *huge*.

> A daring life comes head-to-head with being online, with mean world syndrome, with being complacent. How can I get off this damn phone?

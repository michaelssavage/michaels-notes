My thoughts on Large Language Models are changing constantly. I'm actually impressed quite often by AI tools, but there's the ever-present feeling that it's destroying the world. I'm not alone in my feelings about the future of AI, as there are daily debates on the tech website Hacker News and on social media like Twitter. <Anchor link="https://news.gallup.com/poll/708224/gen-adoption-steady-skepticism-climbs.aspx" text="This article" variant="link" isExternal /> from Gallup writes "While a steady 51% of Gen Zers in the U.S. report using generative artificial intelligence at least weekly, negative emotions toward it have intensified over the past year. Anger about the technology has risen, while excitement and hopefulness have dropped".

This page will serve as a living, breathing document that I'll update with my own feelings, and with comments I've found interesting. 

AI can make you go faster. But sometimes you go fast in the wrong direction and it causes more issues than not.

Over reliance on the *black box of bullshit* is going to become such a disaster in a way no one would have thought possible. Not sure exactly what, who, when, or where... Just that having your entire product or repo dependent on a single entity is going to lead to some bad times...

AI is allowing new beginners to become developers and create projects. But it's breaking the process that allowed developers to create intricate work like LLMs. It's preventing the middle rung in the ladder of development. Engineers still need to learn to program, learn to create plans, and organize code.

The whole thing is a money racket that happens to be useful now and again. Seven years ago, OpenAI said that their new natural language model, GPT-2, was too dangerous to release.

Coding agents is just gambling. You get a fantastic result once in a while and you think they're magical. You end up chasing that high, tweaking the prompt parameters to take on bigger tasks and creating more chaotic superstitions.

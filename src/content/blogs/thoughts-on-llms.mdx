> AI can make you go faster. But sometimes you go fast in the wrong direction and it causes more issues than not.

My thoughts on Large Language Models (LLMs) are changing constantly. I'm actually impressed quite often by Generative AI tools, but there's the ever-present feeling that it's destroying the world. I'm not alone in my feelings about the future of AI, as there are daily debates on Reddit, Twitter, and tech website Hacker News. 

<Anchor link="https://news.gallup.com/poll/708224/gen-adoption-steady-skepticism-climbs.aspx" text="This article" variant="link" isExternal /> from Gallup writes "While a steady 51% of Gen Zers in the U.S. report using generative artificial intelligence at least weekly, negative emotions toward it have intensified over the past year. Anger about the technology has risen, while excitement and hopefulness have dropped". I use AI daily for work. I use Cursor as a code editor, Claude for agentic coding tasks, and ChatGPT from time to time for general purpose tasks. Late in 2025, the Harvard Gazette quoted an <Anchor link="https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2025/11/is-ai-dulling-our-minds/" text="MIT Media Lab study" variant="link" isExternal />, reporting that “excessive reliance on AI-driven solutions” may contribute to “cognitive atrophy” and shrinking of critical thinking abilities. 

This page will serve as a living, breathing document that I'll update with my own feelings, and with comments I've found interesting. A document to reclaim my thinking.

> Over reliance on the *black box of bullshit* is going to become such a disaster in a way no one would have thought possible. Not sure exactly what, who, when, or where... Just that having your entire product or repo dependent on a single entity is going to lead to some bad times...

> AI is allowing new beginners to become developers and create projects. But it's breaking the process that allowed developers to create intricate work like LLMs. It's preventing the middle rung in the ladder of development. Engineers still need to learn to program, learn to create plans, and organize code.

> The whole thing is a money racket that happens to be useful now and again. Seven years ago, OpenAI said that their new natural language model, GPT-2, was too dangerous to release.

> Coding agents is just gambling. You get a fantastic result once in a while and you think they're magical. You end up chasing that high, tweaking the prompt parameters to take on bigger tasks and creating more chaotic superstitions.
